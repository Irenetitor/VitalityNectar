The Largest Smoothie:

Did you know that the largest smoothie ever created was a whopping 1064.61 US gallons (4030 liters)? It was made by Stichting Jaarmarkt from the Netherlands and included ingredients like strawberries, honey, orange juice, bananas, yogurt, sugar, and ice.

Historical Roots:

Smoothies have been around for quite some time. Mediterranean and Eastern cultures were the first to create these delicious pureed fruit drinks. While our modern versions may look different, we owe them credit for inspiring our favorite fruity concoctions.

Global Appeal:

Blending fruits with ice and other ingredients is common in many countries. For example, in Latin America and South America, you’ll find a variety of fruit juice concoctions with exotic fruits. In India, the ancient “lassi” made with yogurt, water or milk, and various seasonings has been enjoyed since around 1000 BC. And don’t forget the Iranian “Sharbat,” popular in South Asia, Caucasus, Turkey, and the Balkans, made from fruit or flower petals and served chilled.

Evolution:

Smoothies have come a long way. From simple fruit, fruit juice, and ice versions in the 1930s to the rise of smoothie bars and pre-bottled options, they’ve become a household favorite. Today, you can find smoothie recipes made with a myriad of ingredients to suit any taste.
